Ejective Consonants — extra "pop" of air
ት vs ጥ — plain T vs ejective T
ት ti plain "t"
ጥ ṭi ejective "t!"
The ejective (ጥ) is like saying "t" while holding your breath out — a sharp, popping sound. Found in words like ጥሩ (good).
ቅ vs ቀ vs ካ — Q sounds
ቅ qi ejective "k!"
ኪ ki plain "k"
ቅ is a forceful k from deep in the throat with sudden release. Try: ቀልጢፍ (fast) vs ኪሎ (kilo).
ጸ vs ጽ vs ፅ — TS ejective family
ጸ tse ejective ts
ጽ tsi ejective tsi
Both are ejective "ts" sounds — found in ጽቡቕ (good/beautiful) and ጸሊም (black).
Pharyngeal / Guttural Sounds
ሀ vs ሓ vs ሃ — H family
ሀ ha soft "h"
ሓ ḥa deep pharyngeal "H"
ሓ is produced deep in the throat — like clearing your throat gently. Found in ሓወቦ (uncle), ሓቀኛ (honest).
አ vs ዐ — glottal vs pharyngeal A
አ a glottal "a"
ዐ 'a voiced pharyngeal
ዐ is like "a" but with a deep, squeezed throat constriction — common in Arabic "ع". Found in ዐይኒ (eye), ዓዲ (village).
Vowel Order (ዘርዓ)
The 7 vowel forms — every consonant has these
ሀ ä
ሁ u
ሂ i
ሃ a
ሄ e
ህ ə
ሆ o
Each Ge'ez consonant has 7 vowel forms. The base consonant "ሀ" (h) becomes ሀ·ሁ·ሂ·ሃ·ሄ·ህ·ሆ — the shape changes to encode the vowel.
Sentence Structure (SOV)
Tigrinya is a Subject–Object–Verb language. The verb comes at the end.
ኣነ ማይ ይሰቲ
Ane may yiseti
I water drink → I drink water
ንሱ ኢንጀራ ይበልዕ
Nisu injera yible'
He injera eats → He eats injera

Negation
Wrap the verb with ኣይ…ን (ay…n) to make it negative.
ኣይ ብለዕ ን
Ay ible' n
I don't eat
ኣይ ኢየ ን
Ay iye n
I am not
